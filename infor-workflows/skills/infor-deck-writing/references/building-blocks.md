# Cross-Cutting Slide-Type Recipes

These slide types appear in more than one deliverable (CIM, pitch, teaser, fairness opinion). Load this file when drafting any of the slide types below — they're the same regardless of which deck they're going into. For deliverable-specific structure (e.g., teaser cover line, fairness opinion conclusion), see the per-deliverable recipe files.

---

## Investment Highlights — Core and Expanded

**Purpose:** The investment thesis. Always presented as 5–6 numbered items. Often appears twice — once as "Core" (one-line punchline each) and once as "Expanded" (each item gets a full slide).

**Structure — Core Investment Highlights (single page):**
```
1. [Positioning headline]
   - 1–2 sub-bullets, ~25–40 words each, semi-colon chained

2. [Positioning headline]
   - 1–2 sub-bullets

… (5–6 items)
```

**Positioning headline pattern menu:**
- `Market Leader Driven by Robust Brand Equity`
- `Well Positioned for Strong Industry Tailwinds`
- `Recession-Proof, Non-Discretionary Offering`
- `Deep Pool of Skilled Labour`
- `Experienced, Technical Management Team`
- `Near-Term Actionable Growth Opportunities in a Fragmented Market`
- `Competitive Moat via Differentiated Platform`
- `Predictable & Profitable Model`
- `Sustainable Supply & Regenerative Farming`
- `Top-Tier Origination Platform`
- `Proprietary Fractionation Process`
- `Unique "Clean Taste" Technology`

Each headline is a noun-phrase, Title Case, 4–8 words. Pair an adjective stack with a noun, or two paired concepts joined with `via`, `&`, or `with`.

**Sub-bullet examples (verbatim):**

> "Iconic specialty automotive brand with 60 years of operating history and a national presence; scarcity value in a network of this size, scale and diversity; natural barriers to entry and high cash flow conversion"

> "Increasing vehicle base (26M on the road), average price of new vehicles (65% increase since 2019), average age (10.3 years) / car parc life (20 years) and complexity of repairs combine to drive demand for specialized services"

> "Long-standing relationships with federal agencies, universities, healthcare systems and major developers, supported by 60+ institutional energy partnerships and tier 1 bidder status"

> "Direct sales effort expansion, unlocking large existing partnerships while launching new partnerships, and other identified cost savings initiatives will drive top line growth and margin expansion"

**Structure — Expanded Investment Highlight (one slide per item):**
```
Title bar: "Investment Highlights"
Sub-title or section header: the positioning headline (#X out of 5–6)
Left column: 2–4 dense bullets developing the thesis
Right column: a supporting visual — chart, table, customer logos, comparison block,
              or a callout box with a stat
Footer: source attribution, currency note
```

The right-column visual is non-negotiable on Expanded pages — see [`../../brand-guidelines-infor/references/deck-template.md`](../../brand-guidelines-infor/references/deck-template.md) for the rationale.

## Company Overview / Business Description

**Purpose:** The company's history, what it does, scale, segments, customers. 1–3 pages in a CIM, 1 page in a teaser, 1 page in a pitch.

**Structure (standard 2-column page):**
- Left column header: `Overview` (section header in navy box)
- Left column: 3–5 bullets describing the Company
- Right column header: `Capitalization Summary` / `Key Figures` / `Select Customers`
- Right column: KPI tiles, a small ownership table, or logo grid

**Opening sentence patterns:**

> Founded in [year] by [founder], [Company] is a leading [sector] provider of [product/service] [geography].

> The Company is a leading [adjective] [sector] [firm/platform/provider] providing [offering] with [scale anchor — e.g., 60+ years of operational excellence, 1,800+ funded transactions, 7,200+ organizations].

> [Company], based in [city], is a [cloud-based / asset-light / SaaS / vertically integrated] [sector] [type of business] that enables its customers to [primary value proposition].

**Verbatim examples:**

> "Founded by Morley Greene in 1997, Trez Capital ("Trez" or the Company") is a leading diversified real estate investment firm and preeminent provider of commercial real estate debt and equity financing solutions across North America. Trez has built a reputation for providing innovative financing for commercial properties (with a focus on multi family residential and lot development) in major centres throughout Canada and the U.S."

> "Kinaxis, based in Ottawa, is a cloud-based supply-chain subscription software provider that enables its customers to improve and accelerate their analysis and decision-making across their supply chain operations."

> "The Company operates as a nationwide network of unified regional offices enabling seamless service delivery with local expertise and touch, while providing expert knowledge and the sharing of resources and best practices."

**Common follow-on bullets:**
- Geographic footprint: `The Company operates [N] offices across [geography]…`
- Customer base: `The Company serves a diverse global customer base, including Fortune 500 companies, government agencies, large insurance providers and other healthcare technology firms; ~XX% of ARR is derived from [region]…`
- Workforce: `The Company currently operates out of [N] offices with ~[N] full-time employees strategically located across North America`
- Track record: `[Company] has funded over [N] transactions totaling more than $[X] billion CAD since inception`

## Business Segments / "What We Do"

**Purpose:** Break out 2–3 business lines in parallel. Each segment gets a description + 3–5 service bullets + revenue mix.

**Structure (typical 2-column or quadrant layout):**
- Top of column: segment name in navy section header (e.g., `MEP Engineering`, `Energy Performance Contracting ("EPC")`)
- Short descriptor under header: 1 line, sets context
- 3–5 named service bullets: `**Engineering & Design** — MEP design, compliance and optimization`
- Revenue or customer split below

**Verbatim examples:**

> **MEP Division:** Functions as a fully integrated mechanical and electrical engineering practice, delivering end-to-end design and advisory services that support clients across a wide range of building types and infrastructure projects

> **EPC Division:** Develops and implements sustainable energy projects, with a focus on materially reducing long-term operating costs and carbon emissions for institutional, commercial and public sector partners

> **EHS:** Provides occupational health, absence management, mental health coaching, EFAP, iCBT, virtual care, assessments and rehabilitation services

> **Engineering & Design** — MEP design, compliance and optimization

> **Project Management** — Planning, coordination and delivery of complex projects

**Pattern for named service bullets:** `**[2–4-word service name]** — [verb-noun phrase or noun phrase, 6–12 words]`

## Industry / Market Overview

**Purpose:** Establish the macro thesis — market size, growth rate, structural drivers, regulatory / demographic tailwinds, competitive landscape.

**Structure:**
- Title: `Market Opportunity`, `Industry Overview`, `Favourable Industry Dynamics Support Growth`, `Market Size (North America)`, or a custom positioning headline
- Lead-in sentence: size + growth rate + horizon
- 3–5 driver bullets: each names a trend and quantifies it
- Optional: a chart (TAM/SAM, growth curve, fragmentation pie) or competitive map (Gartner-style)
- Source attribution at bottom (Bloomberg, equity research, third-party reports)

**Lead-in sentence patterns:**

> The [sector] market is projected to grow from US$[X]B in [year] to US$[Y]B in [year], implying a [Z]% CAGR.

> Over [X]% of [country] [sector] firms have fewer than [N] employees, creating opportunity for scalable platforms to consolidate market share and win larger mandates.

> Following the [event/regulation], [trend] has accelerated, with employer-provided [X] costs forecasted to increase ~[Y]% by [year].

**Verbatim examples:**

> "Over 85% of Canadian engineering consulting firms have fewer than 20 employees, creating opportunity for scalable platforms to consolidate market share and win larger mandates. The Canadian engineering services market is projected to grow from US$115B in 2023 to US$164B in 2030."

> "Government spending on infrastructure continues to underpin demand, supported by the existing C$180B Investing in Canada Plan and the new C$3B per year Public Transit Program (launching in 2026)."

> "Digital health solutions are essential in retaining / engaging workers; ~2/3rds of employers plan to increase spending on mental health in next 5 years. In the U.S., mental illness leads to ~US$193B in lost earnings / productivity annually; the Company is well positioned to capture more of this market as it executes its multi-pronged go to market strategy."

**Pitfalls:**
- Never cite "[Company] estimates" alone for market size — pair with a third-party source.
- Don't write 7+ driver bullets — keep it to 3–5 well-supported drivers.
- If using a Gartner-style 2×2, the axis labels and quadrant names go in the navy boxes; the company logos go in the four quadrants — see [`../../brand-guidelines-infor/references/deck-template.md`](../../brand-guidelines-infor/references/deck-template.md) sample slide #6.

## Process — Key Process Milestones / Timeline

**Purpose:** Lay out the transaction timeline and what each step requires of bidders. Standard CIM section.

**Structure:**
- Horizontal swimlane or numbered table of milestones
- Each milestone has: Phase name, Date range, Activities, Bidder obligations
- Below: a paragraph or 3–4 bullets describing how INFOR will manage the process

**Phase names — standard set:**
1. Initial Outreach & Confidentiality Agreement
2. CIM Distribution & Preliminary Diligence
3. Expression of Interest (EOI) / Indication of Interest (IOI)
4. Management Presentations & VDR Access
5. Confirmatory Due Diligence
6. Final Bid / Letter of Intent (LOI)
7. Share Purchase Agreement Negotiation
8. Signing & Close

**Verbatim bullets:**

> "INFOR has commenced outreach to select strategic acquirers and litigation finance providers to gauge interest in a potential transaction"

> "INFOR has prepared a data room for review by prospective parties in their preliminary diligence of [the Company]"

> "INFOR expects to solicit non-binding EOIs from qualified parties following initial diligence, with flexibility on transaction structure"

> "The Process will be managed in accordance with procedures that will be established and communicated to interested parties from time to time by IFI"

## Buyer-List Commentary

**Purpose:** Introduce the strategic / financial / other-buyer pages. The actual buyer list table is built via [`buyerslist-infor`](../../buyerslist-infor/SKILL.md); this skill writes the commentary.

**Page structure:**
- Title: `Select Potential Acquirors – Strategic Acquirors`, `Select Potential Acquirors – Financial Sponsors`, or a custom group name
- 1-paragraph intro (3–5 sentences) explaining the rationale for the group
- The buyer table below (built separately)
- 1-line note at the bottom explaining tiering or selection criteria

**Intro paragraph — verbatim examples:**

> "In addition to financial sponsors, we believe there are numerous potential strategic acquirors that are familiar with and will appreciate the Company's business, which may drive a premium valuation in a potential transaction."

> "INFOR has strong relationships with many large U.S. private equity technology investors which have expressed interest in acquiring a flagship supply chain asset such as Kinaxis."

> "Below we have highlighted a preliminary list of private equity firms that we believe are potential acquirors of [the Company]; this is not intended to be exhaustive, and INFOR is keen to work with management to supplement and further refine this list."

**Pattern:**
1. Sentence 1: position the group (strategic vs financial; geography; rationale)
2. Sentence 2: INFOR's relationships / read on appetite for the asset
3. Sentence 3 (optional): caveat — "not intended to be exhaustive; INFOR will refine with Management"

## Valuation Commentary

### Comparable Companies / Trading Comps

**Purpose:** Introduce the comps table and explain where the target trades relative to peers.

**Structure:**
- Title: `Comparable Companies`
- Table built via [`comps-infor`](../../comps-infor/SKILL.md) (18 tickers, 3 groups, with the target highlighted)
- 1–2 commentary bullets above or below the table

**Commentary bullet patterns:**

> "[Company] is currently trading at a discount to its peers on an EV / Revenue basis with materially lower margins ([Target %] vs. [Peer Median %])."

> "Analysts project a [X]% increase in revenue growth ([rank] amongst peers) and [Y]% in Adj. EBITDA margin ([rank] amongst peers)."

> "Peer median EV / NTM EBITDA of [N.NN]x reflects [characterisation of the peer set — e.g., 'investor appetite for asset-light recurring-revenue businesses']."

### Precedent Transactions

**Structure:** Table built via [`precedents-infor`](../../precedents-infor/SKILL.md). Commentary above or below.

**Commentary bullet patterns:**

> "Precedent transactions in the [sector] sector have traded in a range of [N.N]x–[M.M]x LTM Revenue and [N.N]x–[M.M]x LTM EBITDA over the past [X] years."

> "Recent transactions have demonstrated a premium for [characteristic — e.g., 'recurring-revenue platforms with multi-year contracts', 'scaled regional consolidators', 'asset-light franchised networks']."

### DCF / Discounted Cash Flow

**Structure:** Title `Approach to Value` or `DCF Analysis`; methodology bullets; assumptions table; sensitivity grid; implied value range.

**Methodology paragraph pattern (from Formal Valuation):**

> "In valuing the Common Shares of [Company], INFOR Financial has primarily relied on [N] methodologies as follows: (1) DCF Analysis – We have constructed a [N]-year DCF ([Start date] – [End date]) based on the [Adjusted Forecast / Management Forecast]. The projections consider [drivers]. Forecasted consolidated unlevered free cash flow ("UFCF") discounted at a weighted average cost of capital ("WACC") of [X.XX]–[Y.YY]%. Terminal multiple approach used to calculate terminal value with an EV / EBITDA exit multiple of [N.N]–[M.M]x."

**Assumption bullets:**
- WACC range: `[X.XX]–[Y.YY]%` based on Capital Asset Pricing Model
- Terminal growth or exit multiple: `[N.N]–[M.M]x` LTM EBITDA / Revenue / EBITDA After Rent
- Forecast horizon: explicit `[N]-year` with `Management Forecast through FY[YYYY]`, INFOR extension thereafter
- Sensitivity grid: 5×5 grid of WACC × Exit Multiple, showing implied EV or implied share price

### Football Field / Implied Valuation Summary

**Structure:** Horizontal range chart per methodology, with implied EV or share-price ranges. One commentary bullet under each methodology row.

**Commentary bullets:**

> "Trading Comparables imply an EV range of C$[X]MM–C$[Y]MM based on [N]x–[M]x [Metric]."

> "Precedent Transactions imply an EV range of C$[X]MM–C$[Y]MM based on [N]x–[M]x [Metric]."

> "The Offer Price of C$[X.XX] per share represents an implied [premium / discount] of [Z]% to [the unaffected share price as of {date}], and falls within the upper end of our implied valuation range."

## Management Team / Bios

**Structure:**
- Title: `Management Team` or `Senior Leadership`
- Each bio: photo placeholder + name + title + 2–3 bullets of background
- Bullets cover: years in industry / role, prior companies, education, certifications

**Bullet patterns:**

> "+20 years in the digital wellbeing industry, founded [Company] in [year]"

> "Previously served as [Title] at [Prior Company]; led [achievement — IPO, $XB exit, scale to $YMM ARR]"

> "Began career as [first role] at [first firm]; M&A and securities law"

> "[Degree] from [University]; CFA / CPA / P.Eng. / MBA"

> "Gold medalist of combined LL.B/MBA program at U of T"

**Length budget:** 3–5 bullets per bio, ~8–15 words each. Don't write paragraphs — these are scannable credentials.

## Financial Summary / KPI Tile Blocks

**Purpose:** Single-page financial snapshot with 4–8 KPI tiles + 2–4 trend charts.

**KPI tile structure:**
- **Figure** — large, bold, centred, with units inline: `~C$46MM`, `+13MM`, `~90%`
- **Caption** — 2–4 words below the figure, smaller, regular weight

**Trend chart titles:**
- `Annual Recurring Revenue (1)`
- `Revenue & Margin Net of Content Costs`
- `Adjusted EBITDA & Margin (2)`
- `Unlevered Free Cash Flow (3)`
- `Volume (000s MT) & Capacity Utilization`
- `Monthly Recurring Revenue (C$000's)`
- `Gross Margin (C$/MT)`

Add `(1)`, `(2)` footnote markers to chart titles when there's a definitional caveat (e.g., FX-adjusted, pre-IPO costs, pro-forma adjustments). Footnotes go at the bottom of the slide at 7 pt.

**Top-of-slide note (always present):**

> Note: All figures in C$MM unless indicated otherwise; FYE [Month Day]
> (1) [Definition, e.g., "Reflects FX adjusted ending ARR; 2021A ARR reflects pro forma adjustments for the full year impact of acquisitions"]
> (2) [Definition]
