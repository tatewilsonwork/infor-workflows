# Fairness Opinion & Formal Valuation Recipes

Fairness opinions are heavily legal and follow a defined slide order. The word `fair` is technical — use it only in the defined sense in the Conclusion section.

Formal valuations (per MI 61-101) extend the fairness-opinion structure with explicit valuation ranges and methodology weighting.

For the underlying valuation methodology language (DCF, comps, precedents, football field), see [`building-blocks.md`](building-blocks.md) → Valuation Commentary.

## Introduction (Transaction Summary)

**Pattern:**

> "INFOR Financial Inc. understands that [Company] is contemplating entering into an arrangement agreement (the "Arrangement Agreement") with [Purchaser Legal Name] (the "Purchaser"), an affiliate of [Parent], pursuant to which the Purchaser would acquire all the outstanding common shares in the capital of [Company] (the "Common Shares") […] for cash consideration of $[X.XX] (the "Consideration") per Common Share (the "Transaction")."

> "INFOR Financial has prepared this presentation to summarize the following with respect to the Fairness Opinion:
>   - **Transaction & Company Overview** — a summary of the key terms of the Transaction and relevant information on the Company
>   - **Approach to Fairness** — fundamental value of the Common Shares on an "en-bloc" basis using a range of methodologies, including discounted cash flow analysis, comparable company analysis, precedent transactions analysis, and sum-of-the-parts analysis; additional evaluation of M&A premiums and analyst target prices has been conducted to benchmark analysis
>   - **Fairness Opinion Conclusion** — summary conclusion of the Fairness Opinion"

## Independence

**Pattern (verbatim list of representations):**

> "INFOR Financial is not an insider, associate, or affiliate (as such terms are defined in the Securities Act (Ontario)) of [Company] or the Purchaser and its affiliates…"

> "INFOR Financial has neither provided financial advisory services nor participated in any financings involving [Company] or the Purchaser and its affiliates over the past [N] months."

> "INFOR Financial's fees in relation to the Fairness Opinion are not contingent on the conclusions reached in our analysis or the success of the Transaction."

> "As such, INFOR Financial believes that it has no conflicts of interest (real or perceived) with regard to the Interested Parties and is, in accordance with MI 61-101 and the related companion policy, independent from the Purchaser (and its affiliates) and the Company."

>   - That all forecasts, projections, estimates and budgets provided to us reflect the best currently available information and estimates as to the matters covered thereby
>   - That since the date INFOR Financial received such information, there has been no material change in the financial condition, assets, liabilities, business, operations or prospects of the Company
>   - […]"

## Scope of Review

A list of every document reviewed, prefaced by:

> "In connection with the Fairness Opinion, INFOR Financial reviewed and relied upon (without attempting to verify independently the completeness or accuracy thereof) or carried out, among other things, the following:"

…followed by a numbered or bulleted list: draft arrangement agreement, draft press release, projected financial information, audited statements, MD&A, AIFs, management circulars, SEDAR filings, certificates from senior officers, research reports, public information on the Company and comparables, etc.

## Approach to Determine Fairness

> "In preparing the Fairness Opinion, INFOR Financial analyzed the Company from a financial perspective, which included, but was not limited to the following methodologies:
>   - Discounted cash flow ("DCF") analysis;
>   - Comparable company analysis;
>   - Precedent transactions analysis;
>   - Sum-of-the-parts ("SOTP") analysis; and
>   - M&A premiums analysis"

For the underlying methodology language (DCF assumptions, comps commentary, precedents commentary, football field), see [`building-blocks.md`](building-blocks.md) → Valuation Commentary.

## Fairness Opinion Conclusion (the actual opinion statement)

**Pattern:**

> "Based upon and subject to the foregoing, and such other matters as we considered relevant, INFOR Financial is of the opinion that, as of the date hereof, the Consideration of $[X.XX] per Common Share to be received by the Common Shareholders (other than the [Excluded Holders]) pursuant to the Transaction is fair, from a financial point of view, to the Common Shareholders (other than the [Excluded Holders])."

The word `fair` is technical — use it only in this defined sense.

## Formal Valuation — Additional Language

Formal valuations (per MI 61-101) require more explicit valuation ranges. Add to the fairness-opinion patterns above:

### Valuation Date

> "Assumes a valuation date of [Month Day, Year]."

### Methodology Weighting

> "INFOR Financial has primarily relied on the following methodologies, weighted as follows: DCF Analysis (primary), Comparable Company Analysis (corroborative), Precedent Transactions Analysis (corroborative)."

### Implied Range

> "Based on the foregoing, INFOR Financial is of the opinion that the fair market value of the Common Shares of [Company], as at [valuation date], is in the range of $[X.XX] to $[Y.YY] per Common Share."
