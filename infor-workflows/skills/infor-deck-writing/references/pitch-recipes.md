# Pitch Recipes

The Pitch is INFOR's advisory document — used to win or extend an engagement with a Board / Special Committee / Management. Confident, advisor-voice, references INFOR's track record and approach.

Most slide types referenced here (Buyer-List Commentary, Valuation Commentary, Industry / Market, Process Milestones) are cross-cutting — load [`building-blocks.md`](building-blocks.md) for those. This file covers the pitch-specific structure.

---

## Pitch — INFOR Firm Overview

**Purpose:** Slide 2–3 of every pitch. Establishes INFOR's credentials.

**Standard wording:**

> "INFOR is Canada's leading provider of innovative, independent, forward-thinking financial and strategic advice."

**Track record bullet pattern:**

> "INFOR has completed M&A with a total transaction value of over $29 billion and we rank first in M&A among independent advisory investment banks in Canada since inception in early 2015."

> "Source: Bloomberg M&A League Tables (Jan. 1, 2024 – Dec. 31, 2024)"

Update the dollar figure, league-table source, and time period to the most recent published numbers when drafting. If unsure of the current number, ask the user.

## Pitch — Buyer Approach Strategy

**Purpose:** Compare General Auction / Focused Auction / Rifle Shot approaches and recommend one for the Transaction.

**Structure (table):**

| Approach | General Auction | Focused Auction | Rifle Shot |
|----------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| **Number of buyers** | 30+ | 10–20 | 1–3 |
| **Confidentiality** | Lower (wider reach) | Medium | Highest (most controlled) |
| **Value maximisation** | Highest competitive tension | Strong tension, manageable | Limited — depends on the one party |
| **Timing** | Longer (3–6 months) | Standard (3–4 months) | Faster (1–3 months) |
| **Business disruption** | Highest | Medium | Lowest |
| **Best for** | Strong asset, broad buyer universe | Defined buyer set, sensitive to confidentiality | Strong inbound interest from a specific party |

**Recommendation paragraph pattern:**

> Based on the Company's [attribute X] and a manageable universe of [N] qualified [strategic/financial] buyers, we recommend a [Focused Auction / General Auction] approach. This approach balances [value maximisation / confidentiality / business disruption] while preserving competitive tension.

## Pitch — Key Risks and Mitigants

**Purpose:** A defensive slide showing INFOR has thought about what might worry a buyer / investor and how to mitigate each.

**Structure (table):**

| Potential Perceived Risks | Mitigants | Diligence Required |
|---------------------------|-----------|--------------------|
| [Concise risk statement, 8–15 words] | [How INFOR/Company will address it] | [What due-diligence work confirms the mitigant] |

**Verbatim row example (constructed pattern):**

| Customer concentration — top 5 customers represent ~50% of revenue | Long-standing relationships (10+ years average), multi-year contracts with renewal terms, deep operational integration | VDR includes contract abstracts, churn analysis, customer references |

**Pattern:** Risks are stated neutrally (not hidden, not amplified). Mitigants are concrete and verifiable. Diligence-required column shows there is evidence to support the mitigant.

## Pitch — Illustrative Timeline & Key Workstreams

**Purpose:** Visual Gantt-style timeline showing the workstreams INFOR will run over the engagement.

**Standard workstreams:**
- Preparation: management diligence, financial model, CIM drafting, marketing materials
- Outreach: NDAs, CIM distribution, EOI solicitation, management presentations
- Bid Stage: IOI/LOI, VDR diligence, site visits, expert sessions
- Documentation: SPA negotiation, regulatory approvals, signing & close

**Header paragraph pattern:**

> "INFOR would continue to work with management to advance our understanding of [the Company]'s business and prepare the required outreach materials. The illustrative timeline below assumes a Process kick-off in [Month YYYY] and signing in [Month YYYY], subject to market conditions and Management availability."

## Pitch — Summary and Near-Term Next Steps

**Purpose:** Closing slide. Reiterates fit and lists 3–5 concrete next steps.

**Verbatim closing patterns:**

> "We will dedicate the full resources of our firm to drive a successful outcome for [Company]; this type of transaction is precisely within our domain of expertise."

> "We look forward to working with the Board / Special Committee / Management to advance the Transaction."

**Next-step bullet pattern:**
- Engagement letter execution
- Completion of preparatory diligence (~[N] weeks)
- CIM and marketing materials finalisation
- Outreach launch in [Month YYYY]
- EOI deadline in [Month YYYY]
