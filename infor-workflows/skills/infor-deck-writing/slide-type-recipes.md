# INFOR Slide-Type Recipes

Per-slide-type templates, structure, opening lines, and verbatim examples drawn from the INFOR deck corpus (CIMs, pitch books, teasers, fairness opinions, formal valuations, strategic reviews).

Each recipe has:
- **Purpose** — what the slide is for
- **Structure** — how INFOR lays out the text
- **Opening line patterns** — verbatim sentence skeletons to adapt
- **Verbatim examples** — real INFOR passages, with the deck noted
- **Length budget** — what to aim for
- **Common pitfalls** — what to cut

Use this together with [`SKILL.md`](SKILL.md) — the parent file covers tone, vocabulary, numbers, and formatting that apply across every slide type.

---

## Teaser — Cover line

**Purpose:** The two-line title under the company name on the teaser cover. The first line is the Project name, the second is a 6–14 word positioning headline.

**Structure:**
```
Project [Codename]
[Positioning headline — 6–14 words, Title Case, no period]
```

**Verbatim examples:**
- `Project Lightning` / `Leading B2B SaaS Digital Wellbeing Platform`
- `Project Meridian` / `Leading Engineering Services Platform`
- `Project Daytona` / `Investment Opportunity in Disruptive Plant Protein Production`
- `Project Thunderbird` / `Specialty Automotive Services Franchisor`
- `Project Meridian` / `AI-Driven Legal Platform with Rare Caselaw Backfile Upside Post-Litigation`

**Pattern menu for the headline:**
- `Leading [adjective] [sector] Platform`
- `[Sector] Leader with [Differentiator]`
- `Investment Opportunity in [Disruptive / Resilient / High-Growth] [Sector]`
- `Differentiated [Sector] Platform with [Specific Edge]`

**Pitfalls:** Don't include the city, the year, or the deal size in the cover line. Don't use a question. Don't include the actual company name (the project codename obfuscates it).

## Teaser — "The Opportunity" (page 1)

**Purpose:** A single page that summarises the company, the transaction, key figures, and 4–5 investment highlights. The first thing a buyer reads.

**Structure (literally in this order):**
1. **Engagement line** at top, defining the Company and the Transaction
2. **Company Overview** — 3–5 sentence paragraph
3. **Key Figures** — 5–6 KPI tiles (large number + 2-word caption)
4. **Investment Highlights** — 4–6 numbered or headline-bulleted items, each with a 4–8 word positioning header and 1–3 supporting bullets

**Engagement line — verbatim skeletons:**

> Project Lightning (the "Company") has engaged INFOR as its exclusive financial advisor in connection with exploring financing alternatives which may result in: i) a financing through the issuance of new equity securities, ii) refinancing the Company's existing debt package, or iii) a combination of the above.

> Faba Canada Ltd. ("Faba Canada" or the "Company") has engaged INFOR Financial Inc. ("INFOR") as its exclusive financial advisor in connection with a potential capital raise of C$25MM, enabling the Company to execute on its growth plan via the build out of the Company's facility, secure required human capital and support working capital.

> Project Meridian (the "Company") has engaged INFOR Financial Inc. ("INFOR") as its exclusive financial advisor in connection with a potential majority divestiture of the shares of the Company (the "Transaction"). The purpose of this Teaser is to provide an overview description of the Company to assist potential buyers in their consideration of a submission of an Expression of Interest ("EOI").

Fill in:
- Project codename or real name
- Defined short term
- Transaction type — sale / majority divestiture / minority investment / capital raise of C$XXMM / financing / recapitalisation / strategic review
- Optional "(the Transaction)" defined term
- Optional purpose-of-teaser sentence

**Company Overview paragraph — verbatim examples:**

> Located in Edmonton, AB, Faba Canada has created a portfolio of superior plant-based protein and co-products from faba bean for the food industry; these new ingredients solve taste, allergenic and functional challenges food companies have in using soy, wheat and pea alternatives. The Company is complete with a production facility, established supply relationships, key industry partners and full food certification; the process is modular, and the Company is ready to scale production to participate in the healthy eating, high protein diet market.

> The Company is a leading software-as-a-service ("SaaS") provider of whole-person wellbeing solutions for employers, health plans and other organizations. The Company's holistic product suite of digital solutions allows organizations to provide best-in-class content and expertise at scale, empowering their employees to live their healthiest lives.

> The Company is a leading Canadian MEP firm providing fully-integrated mechanical and electrical engineering services and energy performance contracting ("EPC") with 60+ years of operational excellence.

**Recipe — Company Overview paragraph:**
1. Sentence 1: location anchor (city / country) + "leading" positioning + sector + product/service
2. Sentence 2: what the platform does or what differentiator it has, with one defined acronym if useful
3. Sentence 3 (optional): the scale anchor — number of customers, employees, revenue, history
4. Sentence 4 (optional): the immediate growth lever or strategic positioning

**Investment-highlight item — verbatim examples (positioning header + supporting bullets):**

> **Competitive Moat via Differentiated Platform**
> - Unique all-in-one wellbeing platform that covers all key modalities with limited competition
> - Combination of scalable technology, vast proprietary content and unique customer service model results in strong user engagement and customer retention

> **Favourable Industry Dynamics Support Growth**
> - Government-led infrastructure investment and sustainability mandates are driving increased need for engineering design and EPC services across Canada
> - The Company is well positioned to benefit from rising regulatory standards, carbon reduction targets and public commitments

> **Predictable & Profitable Model**
> - Plays a critical role in connecting / enhancing wellbeing programs and is highly regarded by its customers
> - Solutions delivered via an asset-light business model with multi-year contracts result in strong, consistent margins, with further operating leverage yet to be realized

**KPI tile blocks — examples (figure + caption):**

| Figure | Caption |
|--------|---------|
| `~C$46MM` | `CY2024E ARR` |
| `~C$12MM` | `Run-Rate EBITDA` |
| `~900` | `Unique Customers` |
| `~90%` | `Adj. Net Dollar Retention` |
| `+13MM` | `Lives Supported` |
| `C$106.3MM` | `FY2025A Revenue` |
| `C$17.4MM` | `FY2025A Adj. EBITDA` |
| `8.0%` | `Revenue CAGR` |
| `60,000+` | `Faba Acres in Network` |

Captions are 2–3 words. The figure carries its own scale (`~`, `C$`, `+`, `%`).

**Length budget:** The whole page is ~150–250 words of body text. If you've written more, you've overshot.

## Teaser — Page 2 (Financial Summary, Key Contacts, Disclaimer)

**Purpose:** Round out the teaser with chart placeholders for financial summary, a key-contacts table, "Platform Core Features" or similar callouts, and the disclaimer.

**Structure:**
- Title bar same as page 1 (Project Codename + cover line)
- 4 chart blocks (Revenue, EBITDA & margin, Volume/Capacity, Gross Margin) — each labelled
- **Key Contacts** table — 4 INFOR bankers, name + title + phone + email
- **Platform Core Features** — 4–6 short feature blocks (icon + 2-line description)
- Disclaimer at the bottom (verbatim — see `SKILL.md` Section 4)

**Chart titles — verbatim examples:** `Revenue (C$MM)`, `EBITDA (C$MM) & Margin (%)`, `Volume (000s MT) & Capacity Utilization`, `Gross Margin (C$/MT)`, `Monthly Recurring Revenue (C$000's)`, `ARR Segmentation (FY2025A)`, `Unlevered Free Cash Flow`.

**Platform Core Feature block — verbatim examples:**
> **Scalable SaaS Technology** — Proprietary technology and delivery systems that can be easily deployed as individual modules or as a fully integrated suite; easily configurable by non-technical users

> **Deep Proprietary Database of Content** — Thousands of proprietary resources from subject matter experts covering high-impact topics; wide breadth & depth of easily consumable and compelling resources to drive health outcomes and user engagement

## CIM — Executive Summary

**Purpose:** Multi-page section opening the CIM. Establishes the Transaction context, the Company, and the investment thesis before deeper sections (Investment Highlights, Company Overview, Market, Financials, Process).

**Structure:**
1. **Transaction Overview** — engagement line, purpose of CIM, contact for INFOR, key dates
2. **Key Process Milestones** — bidder timeline (Confidentiality Agreement → EOI → IOI → Management Presentations → LOI → Confirmatory Diligence → SPA → Close)
3. **Company at a Glance** — single-page company snapshot mirroring the teaser page 1
4. **Investment Highlights** — see Investment Highlights recipe below

**Opening line patterns:**

> The Company has engaged INFOR Financial Inc. ("IFI") as its exclusive financial advisor and intermediary in connection with a Transaction with the Company (the "Process").

> Responsive Brands Inc., dba Mister Transmission International ("Mister Transmission", or the "Company"), has engaged INFOR Financial Inc. ("INFOR") as its exclusive financial advisor in connection with a potential divestiture of up to 100% of the shares of the Company (the "Transaction").

> The sole purpose of this Confidential Information Memorandum (the "CIM") is to provide an overview of [the Company] to assist interested parties in evaluating a potential Transaction.

**Process restriction language (verbatim):**
> Interested parties are precluded by the Confidentiality Agreement from having discussions or communications of any kind in respect of [the Company] or the Process with any person.

> Officers, management and employees, customers, vendors and other business partners of the Company shall not be contacted about this opportunity under any circumstances without INFOR's prior written consent.

> The Process will be managed in accordance with procedures that will be established and communicated to interested parties from time to time by IFI.

**Length budget:** Executive Summary section spans 4–8 slides in a CIM. Each individual slide carries 80–250 words of body text.

## Investment Highlights — Core and Expanded

**Purpose:** The investment thesis. Always presented as 5–6 numbered items. Often appears twice — once as "Core" (one-line punchline each) and once as "Expanded" (each item gets a full slide).

**Structure — Core Investment Highlights (single page):**
```
1. [Positioning headline]
   - 1–2 sub-bullets, ~25–40 words each, semi-colon chained

2. [Positioning headline]
   - 1–2 sub-bullets

… (5–6 items)
```

**Positioning headline pattern menu:**
- `Market Leader Driven by Robust Brand Equity`
- `Well Positioned for Strong Industry Tailwinds`
- `Recession-Proof, Non-Discretionary Offering`
- `Deep Pool of Skilled Labour`
- `Experienced, Technical Management Team`
- `Near-Term Actionable Growth Opportunities in a Fragmented Market`
- `Competitive Moat via Differentiated Platform`
- `Predictable & Profitable Model`
- `Sustainable Supply & Regenerative Farming`
- `Top-Tier Origination Platform`
- `Proprietary Fractionation Process`
- `Unique "Clean Taste" Technology`

Each headline is a noun-phrase, Title Case, 4–8 words. Pair an adjective stack with a noun, or two paired concepts joined with `via`, `&`, or `with`.

**Sub-bullet examples (verbatim):**

> "Iconic specialty automotive brand with 60 years of operating history and a national presence; scarcity value in a network of this size, scale and diversity; natural barriers to entry and high cash flow conversion"

> "Increasing vehicle base (26M on the road), average price of new vehicles (65% increase since 2019), average age (10.3 years) / car parc life (20 years) and complexity of repairs combine to drive demand for specialized services"

> "Long-standing relationships with federal agencies, universities, healthcare systems and major developers, supported by 60+ institutional energy partnerships and tier 1 bidder status"

> "Direct sales effort expansion, unlocking large existing partnerships while launching new partnerships, and other identified cost savings initiatives will drive top line growth and margin expansion"

**Structure — Expanded Investment Highlight (one slide per item):**
```
Title bar: "Investment Highlights"
Sub-title or section header: the positioning headline (#X out of 5–6)
Left column: 2–4 dense bullets developing the thesis
Right column: a supporting visual — chart, table, customer logos, comparison block,
              or a callout box with a stat
Footer: source attribution, currency note
```

The right-column visual is non-negotiable on Expanded pages — see `brand-guidelines-infor` Section 11.6 for why.

## Company Overview / Business Description

**Purpose:** The company's history, what it does, scale, segments, customers. 1–3 pages in a CIM, 1 page in a teaser, 1 page in a pitch.

**Structure (standard 2-column page):**
- Left column header: `Overview` (section header in navy box)
- Left column: 3–5 bullets describing the Company
- Right column header: `Capitalization Summary` / `Key Figures` / `Select Customers`
- Right column: KPI tiles, a small ownership table, or logo grid

**Opening sentence patterns:**

> Founded in [year] by [founder], [Company] is a leading [sector] provider of [product/service] [geography].

> The Company is a leading [adjective] [sector] [firm/platform/provider] providing [offering] with [scale anchor — e.g., 60+ years of operational excellence, 1,800+ funded transactions, 7,200+ organizations].

> [Company], based in [city], is a [cloud-based / asset-light / SaaS / vertically integrated] [sector] [type of business] that enables its customers to [primary value proposition].

**Verbatim examples:**

> "Founded by Morley Greene in 1997, Trez Capital ("Trez" or the Company") is a leading diversified real estate investment firm and preeminent provider of commercial real estate debt and equity financing solutions across North America. Trez has built a reputation for providing innovative financing for commercial properties (with a focus on multi family residential and lot development) in major centres throughout Canada and the U.S."

> "Kinaxis, based in Ottawa, is a cloud-based supply-chain subscription software provider that enables its customers to improve and accelerate their analysis and decision-making across their supply chain operations."

> "The Company operates as a nationwide network of unified regional offices enabling seamless service delivery with local expertise and touch, while providing expert knowledge and the sharing of resources and best practices."

**Common follow-on bullets:**
- Geographic footprint: `The Company operates [N] offices across [geography]…`
- Customer base: `The Company serves a diverse global customer base, including Fortune 500 companies, government agencies, large insurance providers and other healthcare technology firms; ~XX% of ARR is derived from [region]…`
- Workforce: `The Company currently operates out of [N] offices with ~[N] full-time employees strategically located across North America`
- Track record: `[Company] has funded over [N] transactions totaling more than $[X] billion CAD since inception`

## Business Segments / "What We Do"

**Purpose:** Break out 2–3 business lines in parallel. Each segment gets a description + 3–5 service bullets + revenue mix.

**Structure (typical 2-column or quadrant layout):**
- Top of column: segment name in navy section header (e.g., `MEP Engineering`, `Energy Performance Contracting ("EPC")`)
- Short descriptor under header: 1 line, sets context
- 3–5 named service bullets: `**Engineering & Design** — MEP design, compliance and optimization`
- Revenue or customer split below

**Verbatim examples:**

> **MEP Division:** Functions as a fully integrated mechanical and electrical engineering practice, delivering end-to-end design and advisory services that support clients across a wide range of building types and infrastructure projects

> **EPC Division:** Develops and implements sustainable energy projects, with a focus on materially reducing long-term operating costs and carbon emissions for institutional, commercial and public sector partners

> **EHS:** Provides occupational health, absence management, mental health coaching, EFAP, iCBT, virtual care, assessments and rehabilitation services

> **Engineering & Design** — MEP design, compliance and optimization

> **Project Management** — Planning, coordination and delivery of complex projects

**Pattern for named service bullets:** `**[2–4-word service name]** — [verb-noun phrase or noun phrase, 6–12 words]`

## Industry / Market Overview

**Purpose:** Establish the macro thesis — market size, growth rate, structural drivers, regulatory / demographic tailwinds, competitive landscape.

**Structure:**
- Title: `Market Opportunity`, `Industry Overview`, `Favourable Industry Dynamics Support Growth`, `Market Size (North America)`, or a custom positioning headline
- Lead-in sentence: size + growth rate + horizon
- 3–5 driver bullets: each names a trend and quantifies it
- Optional: a chart (TAM/SAM, growth curve, fragmentation pie) or competitive map (Gartner-style)
- Source attribution at bottom (Bloomberg, equity research, third-party reports)

**Lead-in sentence patterns:**

> The [sector] market is projected to grow from US$[X]B in [year] to US$[Y]B in [year], implying a [Z]% CAGR.

> Over [X]% of [country] [sector] firms have fewer than [N] employees, creating opportunity for scalable platforms to consolidate market share and win larger mandates.

> Following the [event/regulation], [trend] has accelerated, with employer-provided [X] costs forecasted to increase ~[Y]% by [year].

**Verbatim examples:**

> "Over 85% of Canadian engineering consulting firms have fewer than 20 employees, creating opportunity for scalable platforms to consolidate market share and win larger mandates. The Canadian engineering services market is projected to grow from US$115B in 2023 to US$164B in 2030."

> "Government spending on infrastructure continues to underpin demand, supported by the existing C$180B Investing in Canada Plan and the new C$3B per year Public Transit Program (launching in 2026)."

> "Digital health solutions are essential in retaining / engaging workers; ~2/3rds of employers plan to increase spending on mental health in next 5 years. In the U.S., mental illness leads to ~US$193B in lost earnings / productivity annually; the Company is well positioned to capture more of this market as it executes its multi-pronged go to market strategy."

**Pitfalls:**
- Never cite "[Company] estimates" alone for market size — pair with a third-party source.
- Don't write 7+ driver bullets — keep it to 3–5 well-supported drivers.
- If using a Gartner-style 2×2, the axis labels and quadrant names go in the navy boxes; the company logos go in the four quadrants — see `brand-guidelines-infor` Section 11.1 sample slide #6.

## Process — Key Process Milestones / Timeline

**Purpose:** Lay out the transaction timeline and what each step requires of bidders. Standard CIM section.

**Structure:**
- Horizontal swimlane or numbered table of milestones
- Each milestone has: Phase name, Date range, Activities, Bidder obligations
- Below: a paragraph or 3–4 bullets describing how INFOR will manage the process

**Phase names — standard set:**
1. Initial Outreach & Confidentiality Agreement
2. CIM Distribution & Preliminary Diligence
3. Expression of Interest (EOI) / Indication of Interest (IOI)
4. Management Presentations & VDR Access
5. Confirmatory Due Diligence
6. Final Bid / Letter of Intent (LOI)
7. Share Purchase Agreement Negotiation
8. Signing & Close

**Verbatim bullets:**

> "INFOR has commenced outreach to select strategic acquirers and litigation finance providers to gauge interest in a potential transaction"

> "INFOR has prepared a data room for review by prospective parties in their preliminary diligence of [the Company]"

> "INFOR expects to solicit non-binding EOIs from qualified parties following initial diligence, with flexibility on transaction structure"

> "The Process will be managed in accordance with procedures that will be established and communicated to interested parties from time to time by IFI"

## Pitch — Buyer Approach Strategy

**Purpose:** Compare General Auction / Focused Auction / Rifle Shot approaches and recommend one for the Transaction.

**Structure (table):**

| Approach | General Auction | Focused Auction | Rifle Shot |
|----------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| **Number of buyers** | 30+ | 10–20 | 1–3 |
| **Confidentiality** | Lower (wider reach) | Medium | Highest (most controlled) |
| **Value maximisation** | Highest competitive tension | Strong tension, manageable | Limited — depends on the one party |
| **Timing** | Longer (3–6 months) | Standard (3–4 months) | Faster (1–3 months) |
| **Business disruption** | Highest | Medium | Lowest |
| **Best for** | Strong asset, broad buyer universe | Defined buyer set, sensitive to confidentiality | Strong inbound interest from a specific party |

**Recommendation paragraph pattern:**

> Based on the Company's [attribute X] and a manageable universe of [N] qualified [strategic/financial] buyers, we recommend a [Focused Auction / General Auction] approach. This approach balances [value maximisation / confidentiality / business disruption] while preserving competitive tension.

## Buyer-List Commentary

**Purpose:** Introduce the strategic / financial / other-buyer pages. The actual buyer list table is built via `buyerslist-infor`; this skill writes the commentary.

**Page structure:**
- Title: `Select Potential Acquirors – Strategic Acquirors`, `Select Potential Acquirors – Financial Sponsors`, or a custom group name
- 1-paragraph intro (3–5 sentences) explaining the rationale for the group
- The buyer table below (built separately)
- 1-line note at the bottom explaining tiering or selection criteria

**Intro paragraph — verbatim examples:**

> "In addition to financial sponsors, we believe there are numerous potential strategic acquirors that are familiar with and will appreciate the Company's business, which may drive a premium valuation in a potential transaction."

> "INFOR has strong relationships with many large U.S. private equity technology investors which have expressed interest in acquiring a flagship supply chain asset such as Kinaxis."

> "Below we have highlighted a preliminary list of private equity firms that we believe are potential acquirors of [the Company]; this is not intended to be exhaustive, and INFOR is keen to work with management to supplement and further refine this list."

**Pattern:**
1. Sentence 1: position the group (strategic vs financial; geography; rationale)
2. Sentence 2: INFOR's relationships / read on appetite for the asset
3. Sentence 3 (optional): caveat — "not intended to be exhaustive; INFOR will refine with Management"

## Pitch — Valuation Commentary

### Comparable Companies / Trading Comps

**Purpose:** Introduce the comps table and explain where the target trades relative to peers.

**Structure:**
- Title: `Comparable Companies`
- Table built via `comps-infor` (18 tickers, 3 groups, with the target highlighted)
- 1–2 commentary bullets above or below the table

**Commentary bullet patterns:**

> "[Company] is currently trading at a discount to its peers on an EV / Revenue basis with materially lower margins ([Target %] vs. [Peer Median %])."

> "Analysts project a [X]% increase in revenue growth ([rank] amongst peers) and [Y]% in Adj. EBITDA margin ([rank] amongst peers)."

> "Peer median EV / NTM EBITDA of [N.NN]x reflects [characterisation of the peer set — e.g., 'investor appetite for asset-light recurring-revenue businesses']."

### Precedent Transactions

**Structure:** Table built via `precedents-infor`. Commentary above or below.

**Commentary bullet patterns:**

> "Precedent transactions in the [sector] sector have traded in a range of [N.N]x–[M.M]x LTM Revenue and [N.N]x–[M.M]x LTM EBITDA over the past [X] years."

> "Recent transactions have demonstrated a premium for [characteristic — e.g., 'recurring-revenue platforms with multi-year contracts', 'scaled regional consolidators', 'asset-light franchised networks']."

### DCF / Discounted Cash Flow

**Structure:** Title `Approach to Value` or `DCF Analysis`; methodology bullets; assumptions table; sensitivity grid; implied value range.

**Methodology paragraph pattern (from Formal Valuation):**

> "In valuing the Common Shares of [Company], INFOR Financial has primarily relied on [N] methodologies as follows: (1) DCF Analysis – We have constructed a [N]-year DCF ([Start date] – [End date]) based on the [Adjusted Forecast / Management Forecast]. The projections consider [drivers]. Forecasted consolidated unlevered free cash flow ("UFCF") discounted at a weighted average cost of capital ("WACC") of [X.XX]–[Y.YY]%. Terminal multiple approach used to calculate terminal value with an EV / EBITDA exit multiple of [N.N]–[M.M]x."

**Assumption bullets:**
- WACC range: `[X.XX]–[Y.YY]%` based on Capital Asset Pricing Model
- Terminal growth or exit multiple: `[N.N]–[M.M]x` LTM EBITDA / Revenue / EBITDA After Rent
- Forecast horizon: explicit `[N]-year` with `Management Forecast through FY[YYYY]`, INFOR extension thereafter
- Sensitivity grid: 5×5 grid of WACC × Exit Multiple, showing implied EV or implied share price

### Football Field / Implied Valuation Summary

**Structure:** Horizontal range chart per methodology, with implied EV or share-price ranges. One commentary bullet under each methodology row.

**Commentary bullets:**

> "Trading Comparables imply an EV range of C$[X]MM–C$[Y]MM based on [N]x–[M]x [Metric]."

> "Precedent Transactions imply an EV range of C$[X]MM–C$[Y]MM based on [N]x–[M]x [Metric]."

> "The Offer Price of C$[X.XX] per share represents an implied [premium / discount] of [Z]% to [the unaffected share price as of {date}], and falls within the upper end of our implied valuation range."

## Fairness Opinion — section-by-section language

Fairness opinions are heavily legal and follow a defined slide order. Use the verbatim wording where shown.

### Cover

```
[Month Year]
[Client Legal Name]
Fairness Opinion Presentation
Private and Confidential
```

### Disclaimer (page 1 — verbatim)

> "The following pages contain material provided to the special committee (the "Special Committee") of the Board of Directors (the "Board") of [Company Legal Name] ("[Company]", or the "Company") by INFOR Financial Inc. ("INFOR Financial", "we", or "us") in connection with the transaction described herein. The accompanying material was compiled or prepared on a confidential basis solely for the use of the Special Committee and the Board. The information contained in the accompanying material was obtained from the Company and other sources. Any estimates and projections contained herein have been prepared or adopted by the management of the Company ("Management"), obtained from public sources, or are based upon such estimates and projections, and involve numerous and significant subjective determinations, and there is no assurance that such estimates and projections will be realized. INFOR Financial does not take any responsibility for such estimates and projections or the basis on which they were prepared. No representation or warranty, express or implied, is made as to the accuracy or completeness of such information…"

(Continue with the standard disclaimer block in the source corpus — it runs ~400 words and is essentially boilerplate. Always include it verbatim; do not paraphrase.)

### Introduction (transaction summary)

**Pattern:**

> "INFOR Financial Inc. understands that [Company] is contemplating entering into an arrangement agreement (the "Arrangement Agreement") with [Purchaser Legal Name] (the "Purchaser"), an affiliate of [Parent], pursuant to which the Purchaser would acquire all the outstanding common shares in the capital of [Company] (the "Common Shares") […] for cash consideration of $[X.XX] (the "Consideration") per Common Share (the "Transaction")."

> "INFOR Financial has prepared this presentation to summarize the following with respect to the Fairness Opinion:
>   - **Transaction & Company Overview** — a summary of the key terms of the Transaction and relevant information on the Company
>   - **Approach to Fairness** — fundamental value of the Common Shares on an "en-bloc" basis using a range of methodologies, including discounted cash flow analysis, comparable company analysis, precedent transactions analysis, and sum-of-the-parts analysis; additional evaluation of M&A premiums and analyst target prices has been conducted to benchmark analysis
>   - **Fairness Opinion Conclusion** — summary conclusion of the Fairness Opinion"

### Independence

**Pattern (verbatim list of representations):**

> "INFOR Financial is not an insider, associate, or affiliate (as such terms are defined in the Securities Act (Ontario)) of [Company] or the Purchaser and its affiliates…"

> "INFOR Financial has neither provided financial advisory services nor participated in any financings involving [Company] or the Purchaser and its affiliates over the past [N] months."

> "INFOR Financial's fees in relation to the Fairness Opinion are not contingent on the conclusions reached in our analysis or the success of the Transaction."

> "As such, INFOR Financial believes that it has no conflicts of interest (real or perceived) with regard to the Interested Parties and is, in accordance with MI 61-101 and the related companion policy, independent from the Purchaser (and its affiliates) and the Company."

### Assumptions & Limitations

**Pattern:**

> "With the Special Committee's approval and agreement, INFOR Financial has relied upon and assumed, among other things:
>   - The completeness, accuracy and fair presentation of all financial and other information (the "Information") provided to us by Management of the Company, its advisors or obtained by INFOR Financial from public sources
>   - That all forecasts, projections, estimates and budgets provided to us are reasonable in the circumstances and consistent with industry practices
>   - That all forecasts, projections, estimates and budgets provided to us reflect the best currently available information and estimates as to the matters covered thereby
>   - That since the date INFOR Financial received such information, there has been no material change in the financial condition, assets, liabilities, business, operations or prospects of the Company
>   - […]"

### Scope of Review

A list of every document reviewed, prefaced by:

> "In connection with the Fairness Opinion, INFOR Financial reviewed and relied upon (without attempting to verify independently the completeness or accuracy thereof) or carried out, among other things, the following:"

…followed by a numbered or bulleted list: draft arrangement agreement, draft press release, projected financial information, audited statements, MD&A, AIFs, management circulars, SEDAR filings, certificates from senior officers, research reports, public information on the Company and comparables, etc.

### Approach to Determine Fairness

> "In preparing the Fairness Opinion, INFOR Financial analyzed the Company from a financial perspective, which included, but was not limited to the following methodologies:
>   - Discounted cash flow ("DCF") analysis;
>   - Comparable company analysis;
>   - Precedent transactions analysis;
>   - Sum-of-the-parts ("SOTP") analysis; and
>   - M&A premiums analysis"

### Fairness Opinion Conclusion (the actual opinion statement)

**Pattern:**

> "Based upon and subject to the foregoing, and such other matters as we considered relevant, INFOR Financial is of the opinion that, as of the date hereof, the Consideration of $[X.XX] per Common Share to be received by the Common Shareholders (other than the [Excluded Holders]) pursuant to the Transaction is fair, from a financial point of view, to the Common Shareholders (other than the [Excluded Holders])."

The word `fair` is technical — use it only in this defined sense.

## Formal Valuation — additional language

Formal valuations (per MI 61-101) require more explicit valuation ranges. Add to the fairness-opinion patterns:

### Valuation Date

> "Assumes a valuation date of [Month Day, Year]."

### Methodology Weighting

> "INFOR Financial has primarily relied on the following methodologies, weighted as follows: DCF Analysis (primary), Comparable Company Analysis (corroborative), Precedent Transactions Analysis (corroborative)."

## Strategic Review — section structure

Strategic reviews lay out the Company's situation and possible paths forward (status quo, M&A, financing, recapitalisation). Less promotional than a CIM, more diagnostic.

**Typical structure:**
1. **Situation Overview** — where the Company stands now (operations, balance sheet, market position)
2. **Strategic Alternatives Considered** — a 2×2 or table comparing options
3. **Detailed Analysis of Each Alternative** — one section per option
4. **Recommendation / Next Steps** — INFOR's recommended path with rationale

**Tone:** Less promotional than CIM. Acknowledges challenges directly. Hedged forward-looking statements throughout.

**Verbatim opening pattern:**

> "INFOR has been engaged by [Company] to undertake a strategic review of the Company's options. This presentation summarises INFOR's analysis of the strategic alternatives available to [Company] and provides recommendations for next steps."

## Editorial workflow — turning raw notes into INFOR slide text

When given raw inputs (call notes, a deck draft, a paragraph of marketing copy, or just a one-line ask), apply this loop:

### 1. Diagnose the slide type

Map the request to one of the recipes in this file. If unsure, ask the user which slide / deck type the text is for.

### 2. Inventory the source

- What **facts** are in the source? (numbers, customer names, milestones)
- What **claims** are supported? (with data, third-party citation, Management quote)
- What is **hype without support**? (cut or replace)
- What's **missing**? (likely needs a follow-up question to the user)

### 3. Draft from the recipe template

Use the opening-line skeleton and structure for the slide type. Fill in the facts. Hedge anything forward-looking.

### 4. Apply the SKILL.md checklist

Run the draft through Section 6 in `SKILL.md` (the style filter). Specifically watch for: Title is a noun phrase, semicolons inside bullets, Canadian spelling, defined-term capitalisation, currency / period notation, AI-style verbs cut, length budget respected.

### 5. Cut

Final pass — strip every word that doesn't earn its place. INFOR copy is dense but never bloated.

### 6. Show the user

Present the draft. If the user asks for variants, generate 2–3 with different positioning headlines or sentence orderings.
