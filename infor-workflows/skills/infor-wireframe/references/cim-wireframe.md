# CIM Wireframe

Slide-by-slide wireframe for an INFOR Financial Group Confidential Information Memorandum (CIM).
Use together with [`SKILL.md`](../SKILL.md). Copy is drafted via
[`infor-deck-writing`](../../infor-deck-writing/SKILL.md); layout and template scaffolding via
[`brand-guidelines-infor`](../../brand-guidelines-infor/SKILL.md).

---

## Header / Defaults

| Item | Default |
|------|---------|
| Page count | **30–80 slides**. Median in the INFOR corpus is ~55. A "slim" CIM (early-stage / capital raise) lands in the 30–40 range; a "full" CIM for a large diversified asset can exceed 70 |
| Cover | A short cover slide carrying `Confidential Information Memorandum` or the Project codename. Real legal name appears only when the Company has authorised an un-anonymised CIM |
| Section system | CIMs navigate via **section dividers** — a single slide carrying the section name in a rounded-rectangle stack with the current section highlighted (see `brand-guidelines-infor` §6.3 and §11.1 sample slide #5). The same section divider can re-appear (or a smaller "tab" version reappears) between sub-sections |
| Numbered sections | Some CIMs prefix section dividers with a number (`1. Executive Summary`, `2. Investment Highlights`); others use the plain section name. Pick one convention and stay consistent within a deck |
| Confidentiality stamp | `Strictly Private & Confidential` on every slide |
| Disclaimer | Page 2 (immediately after the cover). Some CIMs add a separate Forward-Looking Statements page after the Disclaimer |
| Contact / Banker page | Always the last slide (single banker block, e.g., the lead analyst), per `brand-guidelines-infor` §11.6 |

---

## Section Map (standard / full CIM)

| Section | Approx. pages | Notes |
|---------|--------------:|-------|
| Cover + Disclaimer (+ Forward-Looking Statements) | 2–3 | Always first |
| Section 1 — Executive Summary | 4–8 | Includes the Transaction Process and a Company-at-a-Glance page (mirrors teaser p.1) |
| Section 2 — Investment Highlights | 5–8 | One single-page "Core Investment Highlights" overview + 4–6 single-slide "Expanded Investment Highlights" pages |
| Section 3 — Business / Company Overview | 6–14 | Company history, business model, services, customer journey, segments, geography, technology, integration, customers |
| Section 4 — Segment Overview (per segment) | 4–10 per segment | Only present when the Company has 2+ business segments. One sub-section per segment |
| Section 5 — Industry / Market Overview | 2–6 | TAM / SAM, fragmentation, growth drivers, regulatory tailwinds, competitive landscape |
| Section 6 — Human Resources / Org / Management | 1–4 | Org chart(s), employee distribution, management bios (sometimes folded into Executive Summary instead) |
| Section 7 — Financial Overview | 6–12 | Assumptions, revenue breakdown, key financial highlights, income statement, EBITDA adjustments / build, balance sheet, cash flow |
| Section 8 — Appendix | 5–20 | Service-offering deep-dives, M&A targets, project tear-sheets, additional segment financials, balance sheet detail, leased facilities |
| Banker contact (single page) | 1 | Always last |

A slim CIM compresses or omits Sections 4, 6, and 8. An expanded CIM extends the Appendix and may
double Section 4 (multi-segment).

### Cover & Disclaimer

| # | Suggested Title | Purpose | Content Blocks | Layout | Status |

## Per-Section Notes

- **Section dividers** are mandatory between major sections. Each one is a single slide showing
  the rounded-rectangle section stack with the current section highlighted (template sample #5).
  CIMs frequently re-insert a section-divider variant between sub-sections of Section 4 (one per
  segment) — see `brand-guidelines-infor` §6.3.
- **Investment Highlights — Core then Expanded** is the standard pattern: 1 single page with all
  5–6 thesis pillars, then 4–6 full slides each developing one pillar. The number of Expanded
  slides should equal the number of pillars on the Core slide.
- **Customer logo grid** is non-negotiable in any CIM where the Company has named enterprise
  customers — buyers expect to see specific names. If customer names are confidential, use
  industries / customer types as tiles instead.
- **Case studies** appear in every CIM in the corpus where the Company has them. Include 2–4
  unless customer confidentiality prevents it.
- **Financial section** always has KPI tiles, an Income Statement, an EBITDA bridge, and a
  Balance Sheet. Cash flow and segment income statements are optional and tend to appear in
  larger / multi-segment CIMs.
- **Forward-Looking Statements page** is a separate slide in a minority of CIMs in the corpus
  (Trez Capital includes it; CloudMD does not). Include it whenever the CIM contains
  Management-prepared forecasts that go more than ~2 years out.

## Variants

### Slim CIM (30–40 slides)

For early-stage or capital-raise CIMs. Compress to:

- Cover + Disclaimer (no separate FLS)
- Executive Summary (3–4 slides — fold the Management Bio in)
- Investment Highlights — Core only, no Expanded slides (or 2–3 Expanded slides)
- Business Overview (3–5 slides; no Segment section)
- Market Overview (1–2 slides)
- Financial Overview (4–6 slides)
- No Appendix
- Single banker contact

### Expanded CIM (60–80+ slides)

For large, multi-segment, multi-region assets. Add:

- Forward-Looking Statements after Disclaimer
- Full Section 4 with one sub-section per segment
- Per-region overview slides within each segment
- Multi-page customer / project tear-sheet section in the Appendix
- Per-segment income statements in the Appendix

### Real-asset / specialty CIM

Some CIMs (asset managers like Trez Capital) include a `Portfolio & Investor Overview` section
between Business and Financial sections — a Section 3.5 with portfolio composition, geographic
mix, vintage analysis, and investor profile. Add as a separate section divider + 8–15 slides when
the Company is an asset manager or holds a portfolio of operating real assets.

## Confidence Notes

- **Cover / Disclaimer / Banker-contact bookends** — Strongly supported across all 6 example CIMs.
- **Section-divider navigation** — Strongly supported (6/6).
- **Executive Summary section with Transaction Process + Company-at-a-Glance** — Strongly
  supported (6/6).
- **Core + Expanded Investment Highlights pattern** — Strongly supported (5/6; one CIM uses Core
  only because it was a slim variant).
- **Investment-Highlights Expanded slides require a right-column visual** — Strongly supported
  (every Expanded slide observed includes a chart, table, logo grid, or callout box on the
  right).
- **Financial Overview includes KPI tiles + Income Statement + EBITDA bridge + Balance Sheet** —
  Strongly supported (6/6).
- **Forward-Looking Statements as a separate slide** — Situational (~2/6).
- **Per-segment sub-sections in Section 4** — Strongly supported when the Company is
  multi-segment (~3/6 of the example CIMs are multi-segment, and all three use this pattern).
- **Appendix tear-sheet pages for projects / service offerings** — Situational; common in
  services / EPC businesses.
