# Fairness Opinion Wireframe

Slide-by-slide wireframe for an INFOR Financial Group fairness opinion presentation. Use together
with [`SKILL.md`](../SKILL.md). Copy is drafted via
[`infor-deck-writing`](../../infor-deck-writing/SKILL.md) — fairness opinions rely on verbatim
legal language; the Independence, Assumptions & Limitations, Approach to Determine Fairness,
Scope of Review, and Fairness Opinion Conclusion language is mandatory and is reproduced in the
recipes. Layout and template scaffolding via
[`brand-guidelines-infor`](../../brand-guidelines-infor/SKILL.md).

Note: a **formal valuation** (MI 61-101) shares ~80% of its structure with a fairness opinion and
additionally produces an explicit fair-market-value range and methodology weighting. Where the
two diverge, this reference flags the difference.

---

## Header / Defaults

| Item | Default |
|------|---------|
| Page count | **35–50 slides**. Median ~40 |
| Audience | The Special Committee of the Board (most common) or the full Board, in connection with their consideration of a signed or contemplated transaction subject to MI 61-101 |
| Cover | Company **legal name** (always — no Project codename); subtitle `Fairness Opinion Presentation` or `[Month Year] Fairness Opinion Presentation`; `Private and Confidential` |
| Section system | Fairness opinions navigate via a **single repeating section divider** labelled `Introduction` that re-appears between each major analytical block. The `Introduction` divider is the navigation tab for the entire deck; the highlighted rounded-rectangle within the divider changes to indicate the current sub-section |
| Confidentiality stamp | `Strictly Private & Confidential` on every slide |
| Disclaimer | Two-page Disclaimer (Disclaimer + Disclaimer (cont'd)) immediately after the cover, ~5,500 chars total — substantially longer than a pitch disclaimer |
| Contact | Standard Contact page as the final slide. Per `brand-guidelines-infor` §11.6 |
| Verbatim language | The Disclaimer, Independence, Assumptions & Limitations, Approach to Determine Fairness, Scope of Review, and Fairness Opinion Conclusion sections use **verbatim language** drawn from `infor-deck-writing` "Fairness Opinion — section-by-section language". Do not paraphrase |

## Section Map

| Section | Approx. pages | Notes |
|---------|--------------:|-------|
| Cover + Disclaimer | 3 | Cover + 2-page Disclaimer (Disclaimer + Disclaimer (cont'd)) |
| Section 1 — Introduction (boilerplate legal) | 6–7 | Introduction / Independence / Assumptions & Limitations / Approach to Determine Fairness / Scope of Review |
| Section 2 — Transaction & Company Overview | 5–10 | Transaction Overview, Term Sheet, P&L Forecast, Capitalization, Ownership, Trading History, Analyst Consensus |
| Section 3 — Approach to Fairness / Valuation Methodologies | 10–15 | Methodologies table + DCF (Key Assumptions, WACC, Valuation Summary, Sensitivity) + Comparable Companies + Precedent Transactions + (optional) SOTP + M&A Premiums + Implied Share Price |
| Section 4 — Fairness Conclusion | 1 | Single slide carrying the opinion statement |
| Section 5 — Appendix | 0–8 | Supporting analyses (peer benchmarking, business descriptions, transaction costs) — optional |
| Section 6 — INFOR Overview (optional) | 0–5 | INFOR Overview, Team, Track Record — included in some fairness opinions, omitted in others |
| Contact | 1 | Always last |

## Per-Slide Notes

- **The `Introduction` section divider is the navigation tab for the entire deck.** It re-appears
  at the start of every major sub-section (Section 1 Legal Boilerplate, Section 2 Transaction &
  Company, Section 3 Valuation, Section 4 Conclusion, optional Section 5 Appendix, optional
  Section 6 INFOR Overview). The current sub-section is indicated by which rounded rectangle is
  highlighted on the divider slide.
- **Verbatim language slides** — the Disclaimer, Independence, Assumptions & Limitations,
  Approach to Determine Fairness, Scope of Review, and Fairness Opinion Conclusion all use
  verbatim language from `infor-deck-writing` "Fairness Opinion — section-by-section language".
  Do not paraphrase any of them.
- **Methodologies presented** — DCF + Comparable Companies + Precedent Transactions + M&A
  Premiums is the standard 4-methodology slate. Add **SOTP** for diversified businesses with
  materially different segments (e.g., CI Financial: Canadian Wealth Management + U.S. Corient
  segments). Add **Minority Precedent Transactions** for non-control / minority-stake
  transactions (Wellington-Altus). Drop M&A Premiums when the Transaction does not involve a
  publicly traded company.
- **Sensitivity grid** — Always a 5×5 grid of WACC × Exit Multiple (or WACC × Terminal Growth)
  showing implied EV or implied share price. The grid is part of the DCF block.
- **Football-field summary** — Always the penultimate slide of Section 3 (before the Conclusion
  in Section 4). The football field is the visual the Special Committee will fix on — make sure
  the chart shows each methodology's implied range and a vertical line at the Consideration.
- **Section 6 INFOR Overview** — Situational; observed in Wellington-Altus but not in CI
  Financial. Include for newer client relationships where the Special Committee benefits from a
  refresher on INFOR's credentials. Omit for repeat engagements.

## Variant — Formal Valuation (MI 61-101)

A Formal Valuation overlaps ~80% with a fairness opinion. The differences:

- Slide 9 is titled `Approach to Valuation` (not `Approach to Determine Fairness`).
- A `Comparison of Key Operational & Financial Figures` slide is added in Section 2 to compare
  the Company-prepared forecast against street consensus and INFOR's adjusted forecast.
- An `Adjusted Forecast Summary` slide replaces `P&L Forecast Summary` when INFOR has adjusted
  Management's forecast.
- Section 4 splits the conclusion into three slides: `Valuation Conclusion` (fair-market-value
  range — required under MI 61-101), `Approach to Fairness Opinion` (the methodologies used for
  the fairness component), and `Fairness Opinion Conclusion`.
- Methodology weighting is explicit on the `Valuation Methodologies` table (Primary /
  Corroborative).
- The Appendix is usually present and includes WACC build detail, peer benchmarking, and
  transaction-costs analysis.

When the user asks for a formal valuation wireframe, start from this fairness-opinion wireframe
and apply the differences above.

## Confidence Notes

The fairness opinion corpus is smaller (2 fairness opinions + 1 formal valuation = 3 example
decks). All structural patterns below were observed in 3/3 unless noted.

- **Cover / Disclaimer (2 pages) / `Introduction` divider system / Conclusion / Contact
  bookends** — Strongly supported (3/3).
- **Section 1 boilerplate (Introduction / Independence / Assumptions & Limitations / Approach to
  Determine Fairness / Scope of Review)** — Strongly supported (3/3). Order is consistent.
- **Section 2 Transaction & Company Overview structure** — Strongly supported (3/3). Order of
  the Trading History / Analyst Consensus / Forecast slides varies slightly.
- **Section 3 Valuation Methodologies (DCF + Comps + Precedents + M&A Premiums)** — Strongly
  supported (3/3).
- **SOTP analysis for diversified businesses** — Situational (1/3, in the CI Financial deck
  where two distinct segments existed).
- **Minority Precedent Transactions analysis** — Situational (1/3, in the Wellington-Altus deck
  where the Transaction was minority).
- **Implied Share Price football field as the closing slide of Section 3** — Strongly supported
  (3/3).
- **Single-slide Fairness Conclusion using verbatim opinion language** — Strongly supported
  (3/3).
- **Optional Appendix** — Situational (2/3 include it; the third does not).
- **Optional INFOR Overview before Contact** — Situational (1/3; Wellington-Altus includes it).
- **`Introduction` as the single repeating section-divider label** — Strongly supported (3/3).
  This is a fairness-opinion-specific pattern that differs from the CIM / pitch convention of
  using a unique divider label per section.

Because the corpus is small, treat the section-divider naming and the precise order within
Sections 2 and 5 as **provisional** — the patterns observed are consistent across the available
decks but could reasonably vary. Larger structural decisions (the 4-methodology slate, the
verbatim language sections, the single-slide Conclusion) are well-supported.
