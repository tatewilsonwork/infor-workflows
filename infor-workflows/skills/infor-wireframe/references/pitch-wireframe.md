# Pitch Wireframe

Slide-by-slide wireframe for an INFOR Financial Group pitch deck (discussion materials for a
client board, special committee, or management team). Use together with
[`SKILL.md`](../SKILL.md). Copy is drafted via
[`infor-deck-writing`](../../infor-deck-writing/SKILL.md); layout and template scaffolding via
[`brand-guidelines-infor`](../../brand-guidelines-infor/SKILL.md).

---

## Header / Defaults

| Item | Default |
|------|---------|
| Page count | **25–50 slides**. Median in the INFOR corpus is ~38. Slimmer when targeting a single audience meeting (29 slides — Pine Mortgage); fuller when the deck covers M&A advisory + multiple case studies (~45–49 slides — MCW, Trez Capital) |
| Audience | Client board, Special Committee, CEO / CFO, or founders. Voice is **advisor voice** — first-person plural ("we", "our"), references INFOR's track record, recommendations are forward-looking but defended |
| Cover | Real company / asset name on the cover (pitches are almost never anonymised — the client knows who they are). Subtitle is `Confidential Discussion Materials` or `Internal Discussion Materials` |
| Section system | Pitches navigate via **section dividers** identical to CIMs — same rounded-rectangle stack with the current section highlighted (`brand-guidelines-infor` §6.3, template sample #5). The section name is repeated between sub-sections. Common section divider labels: `Executive Summary`, `Capital Markets Perspectives`, `Approach to Value`, `Financial Summary & Approach to Value`, `Canadian Market Dynamics & Take-Private Activity` |
| Confidentiality stamp | `Strictly Private & Confidential` on every slide |
| Disclaimer | Single short disclaimer slide (~1,400 chars — much shorter than the CIM disclaimer) as the second-to-last slide |
| Contact | Standard 4-banker Contact page (template sample #9) as the final slide. Per `brand-guidelines-infor` §11.6 |

## Section Map (standard pitch)

| Section | Approx. pages | Notes |
|---------|--------------:|-------|
| Cover + Executive Summary | 2–4 | 1 cover + 1–3 Executive Summary text slides |
| INFOR Credentials | 4–6 | INFOR Overview, Track Record, Deal Team, References — establishes INFOR's right to win |
| Approach to Value | 8–12 | The valuation work — comps, precedents, DCF, LBO, Implied Valuation. Often introduced by a "Company at a Glance" / "Positioning the Company" / "Key Risks and Mitigants" trio |
| Buyer Universe | 2–4 | Select Potential Acquirors — Financial Sponsors, Strategic Acquirors, sometimes a third category (Investors, U.S. Acquirors). Built via [`buyerslist-infor`](../../buyerslist-infor/SKILL.md) |
| Process & Engagement | 4–6 | Buyer Approach Strategy, Strategic Exit Considerations, Illustrative Timeline, Proposed Terms of Engagement, Summary & Near-Term Next Steps |
| Case Studies & Track Record | 5–15 | Long-Term Relationship case studies, sector case studies, INFOR Transactions tear-sheet pages |
| Disclaimer + Contact | 2 | Always last two slides |

The order of the **INFOR Credentials**, **Approach to Value**, **Buyer Universe**, **Process &
Engagement**, and **Case Studies** sections varies between decks — INFOR sometimes leads with
Credentials, sometimes leads with Approach to Value. Pick the order that matches the audience:
lead with **Credentials** for a new client / beauty parade; lead with **Approach to Value** for
an existing relationship where the analytical work is the headline.

## Per-Section Notes

- **Section dividers are re-used across the pitch.** Unlike the CIM, where every section gets a
  uniquely-labelled divider, pitches commonly use one section-divider label (e.g., `Executive
  Summary`, `Approach to Value`, `Canadian Market Dynamics & Take-Private Activity`) and repeat
  the same divider slide between sub-sections — see the Open Text pitch (`Canadian Market
  Dynamics & Take-Private Activity` divider appears 4 times). The divider acts as a navigation
  tab.
- **Ordering of the major sections varies.** Beauty parade decks lead with **INFOR Credentials**
  → **Approach to Value**. Live-engagement / advisory decks for a known client typically lead
  with **Approach to Value** → **Buyer Universe** → **Process** → **Case Studies**, putting the
  analytical work first. Decide order based on what the audience already knows about INFOR.
- **Capital Markets Perspectives** is an alternate section-divider name used in take-private /
  public-company pitches (Kinaxis, Open Text) — it bundles the Approach-to-Value content with
  public-company-specific slides like Annotated Stock Chart, Turn of Float Analysis, and
  Take-Private Premiums. Use this label instead of `Approach to Value` when the asset is a
  public company being evaluated for take-private.
- **Key Risks and Mitigants** is a table slide and almost always uses the 3-column structure
  (Risk / Mitigant / Diligence Required). 4–8 rows is the corpus norm.
- **LBO analysis** (LBO Model Overview, Leverage Assumptions, Returns / Sensitivity) is included
  only when the recommended path is take-private / sponsor-led. Omit for vanilla strategic-sale
  pitches.
- **Case-Study slides** are dense — each one is effectively a one-page narrative (~2,000–2,500
  characters of body text). Plan one full slide per case study, not a card in a grid.
- **INFOR Transactions tear-sheet pages** appear at the end of every example pitch — between 3
  and 10 slides of 18-transaction tile grids. The number of tear-sheet pages scales with how
  much track record INFOR wants to show.

## Variants

### Slim pitch (25–30 slides)

For a single audience meeting with a known client. Compress to:

- Cover + Executive Summary (1 page)
- INFOR Credentials (3 slides — Overview + Track Record + Team only)
- Approach to Value (5–6 slides — Company at a Glance + Comps + Precedents + Implied Valuation +
  Buyer Approach + Timeline)
- Buyer Universe (1–2 slides)
- 1–2 Case Studies + 1 INFOR Transactions tear-sheet page
- Disclaimer + Contact

### Expanded pitch (45–50 slides)

For a beauty parade or unsolicited proposal. Add:

- Executive Summary (Cont'd)
- Capital Markets Perspectives section (Annotated Stock Chart + Historical Valuation + Analyst
  Consensus + Thematic Analysis) for public companies
- LBO Model Overview + Leverage Assumptions + Returns / Sensitivity for take-private pitches
- Strategic Exit Considerations + Three Pillar Approach
- 5–10 Case Studies (Long-Term Relationships + Sector-specific + Recent transactions)
- 4–6 INFOR Transactions tear-sheet pages

### Public-company take-private pitch

Use `Capital Markets Perspectives` as the section-divider name. Add the public-company analysis
slides between Approach-to-Value and Buyer Universe:

- Annotated Stock Chart
- Historical Valuation Analysis
- Analyst Consensus
- Thematic Analysis — Summary of Analyst Commentary
- Take-Private Considerations
- INFOR's Sponsor Coverage Feedback
- Select Take-Private Transactions in Canada / North American Software Take-Privates

These are the markers that move a vanilla pitch into take-private territory.

## Confidence Notes

- **Cover / Disclaimer / Contact bookends** — Strongly supported (7/7 example pitches).
- **Executive Summary as 1–2 dense text pages** — Strongly supported (7/7).
- **INFOR Credentials section (Overview + Track Record + Team)** — Strongly supported (7/7;
  always present, sometimes labelled differently).
- **Approach to Value section structure (Company at a Glance + Comps + Precedents + Valuation +
  Implied Valuation)** — Strongly supported (7/7).
- **Buyer Universe with Strategic + Financial split** — Strongly supported (6/7; the seventh
  pitch is for a take-private with a single buyer type).
- **Process section (Buyer Approach + Timeline + Engagement Terms + Next Steps)** — Strongly
  supported (7/7).
- **Case Studies + INFOR Transactions tear-sheets at the end** — Strongly supported (7/7).
- **Section-divider repetition (same divider re-used between sub-sections)** — Strongly
  supported (7/7).
- **LBO analysis** — Situational (3/7); always present in take-private pitches and absent
  otherwise.
- **Capital Markets Perspectives section label** — Situational (2/7); used specifically for
  public-company take-private pitches.
